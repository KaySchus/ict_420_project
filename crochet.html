Hooked on Crochet

A Beginner’s Guide

What is Crochet?

It is important to know Yarn Crafts are a large and diverse field, if a topic you believe should be covered is not feel free to investigate on your own.

Crochet is a needlework technique that uses a single hook to interlock loops of yarn, thread, or other materials. Crochet generally involves completing one stitch at a time, leaving only one active loop on the hook.

The Starter Kit

You don't need a craft store's entire inventory to begin. Here are the "must-haves" for a beginner:

  1. Crochet Hook - A size H-8 (5mm) is a very beginner friendly size to work with most budget yarns
  2. Yarn - Any yarn can work but a medium weight (Weight 4 on the package) will work ideally with the above hook
  3. Scissors - Helpful to cut loose strands and end projects
  4. Tapestry Needle - These are large, blunt needles that are used for weaving in the ends of your project to remove loose threads
  5. Stitch Markers - These are small plastic clips that allow you to insert them into a stitch to remember a location or count

The Essential Crochet Stitches

Whether you're just picking up a hook for the first time or need a quick refresher, these stitches form the foundation of almost every crochet pattern. Mastery of these six will allow you to create everything from blankets to sweaters.

Stitch Name Abbreviation Description
Chain Stitch ch The foundation of most projects; it creates a simple loop.
Slip Stitch sl st The shortest stitch, used to join rounds or move yarn across a row without adding height.
Single Crochet sc A short, tight stitch that creates a dense, sturdy fabric - the most simple standard stitch.
Half Double Crochet hdc The middle child; it’s taller than a single crochet but shorter than a double.
Double Crochet dc A tall, versatile stitch that creates a softer drape and works up quickly.
Treble Crochet tc A very tall, "leggy" stitch used for lace, openwork, or adding significant height.
